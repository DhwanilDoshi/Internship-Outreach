## Example 1 — Deep Tech Hardware Company

**Company:** Maritime Fusion (YC W25) — building underwater inductive power transfer systems for marine ROVs
**Contact:** Justin Waugh, Co-founder
**Website scraped text quality:** Good — detailed technical content about underwater robotics

---

### Version A — Professional

**Subject:** Maritime Fusion's inductive power transfer work — internship ask

**Body:**
```
Hi Justin,

Saw the work Maritime Fusion is doing on contactless power coupling for underwater ROVs —
the constraint of maintaining transfer efficiency through seawater is a genuinely hard
electromagnetic problem, and the way you're approaching it with passive resonant circuits
is smart.

I'm Dhwanil, a 2nd-year ECE student at SCET, Surat. My coursework is in signals and
power electronics, and I've been working through underwater acoustic propagation projects
on the side. I think I could contribute meaningfully on the electromagnetic modeling side.

Would you be open to a quick call to see if there's a fit for a remote internship? Happy
to share any relevant work.

— Dhwanil Doshi, ECE (2nd year), SCET Surat
```

**What makes this good:**
- Opener references a specific technical detail (inductive power transfer + seawater + resonant circuits) — shows genuine engagement, not a Google search of the company name
- "Genuinely hard electromagnetic problem" — respects the founder's work without sycophancy
- Second paragraph is specific and brief: signals, power electronics, what he can do
- Ask is clear and low-friction: "a quick call"
- No buzzwords present
- Under 180 words

- "Fascinating" is used as a descriptor of the technical problem, not a buzzword about the company

**Company:** Stackr (YC S25) — spend management platform for growing startups

- Specific skills mentioned (backend systems, data pipelines) that map to the company's actual tech
- Question at the end is specific and invites a yes/no answer

## Patterns That Make These Emails Work

| Pattern | Example | Why It Works |
|---------|---------|--------------|
| **Technical specificity in opener** | "inductive coupling through seawater" | Shows you actually understand the work, not just the company name |
| **Architecture-level observation** | "putting spend visibility inside the approval flow rather than downstream" | Demonstrates business/technical thinking, not just enthusiasm |
| **Honest framing** | "Came across Stackr while looking at YC S25 companies" | Founders know you're doing outreach; pretending otherwise feels manipulative |
| **Short Version B** | 73–93 words | Busy founders read the whole thing. More emails = more responses |
| **Low-friction ask** | "Worth a conversation?" / "Is there capacity?" | Yes/no question is easier to answer than "Please reply with your thoughts" |
| **No grades or GPA** | Absent entirely | Founders don't care about GPA. Projects and thinking matter. |
| **Sign-off with school + year** | "ECE (2nd year), SCET Surat" | Provides context without making it the focus of the email |
